Methodology

How every metric on this dashboard is measured, where it comes from, and how often it refreshes. Read once, trust thereafter.

I · Source of truth
Where the numbers come from

Rankings come from Google Search Console only. After research showed every other rank source (Ahrefs, SEMrush, manual checks) systematically overstates positions for personalised SERPs, GSC's impression-weighted real-user data became the only methodologically defensible source.

Domain Rating, backlinks, referring domains, anchor text, and competitive metrics come from the Ahrefs API v3, refreshed twice weekly (Mondays and Fridays at 00:05 GST).

Organic traffic estimates on Domain Overview come from Ahrefs (estimated). Future versions will replace this with actual GSC click totals once the Apps Script pulls domain-level GSC data.

Core Web Vitals are 28-day field data from Chrome User Experience Report via the PageSpeed Insights API. Pipeline pending.

III · Definitions
What each term means

Best position. The lowest (best) daily position recorded by Google Search Console for the week. If a keyword ranked at position 14 on Monday and position 22 on Tuesday, best position is 14.

Avg position. Impression-weighted average position across the week. More accurate for traffic potential than a simple mean.

Days active. How many days in the week the keyword received at least one impression. A "7 / 7" keyword is consistently visible; a "1 / 7" keyword had a one-day spike.

Domain rating (DR). Ahrefs' measure of domain backlink strength on a 0-100 logarithmic scale. Values move slowly; even one-point increases are meaningful at low DR.

Referring domain. A unique website that has at least one link pointing to your domain. Growing referring domains is generally more valuable than growing total backlinks.
